Human Rights at the Time of COVID-19
Published May 2020
The reality of the COVID-19 pandemic has impacted every country, and poorer ones more than the others. However, an abrupt halt to economic activites tends to overshadow various violations to human rights during this critical time. In this special issue (#3), Francesco Rende explores how Authoritarian governments take advantage of the pandemic to surveil its people, Albert Lou discusses the disproportionate impact of the virus on healthcare workers, and Josh Li investigates the effect of sanctions in deteriorating living conditions of innocent citizens.
